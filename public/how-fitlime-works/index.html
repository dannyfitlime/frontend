How FitLime builds a meal plan tailored to you

Unlike static templates, FitLime does not rely on pre-made tables. It uses a dynamic process in which each personalized meal plan is calculated from scratch. The system works with an algorithm that evaluates dozens of input parameters at once, including basal metabolism, the thermic effect of food, and specific energy expenditure linked to physical activity.

The goal of this nutrient optimization is not just mathematical precision, but above all sustainability in real life. A personalized meal plan must account not only for total calorie intake, but also for preparation time, ingredient availability, and your individual taste preferences.

The final plan ensures that energy balance matches your goal precisely, whether that goal is fat loss, muscle gain, or performance stability. Each day is structured to stay balanced so blood sugar remains stable and you have enough energy for both work and training.

FAQ

Is FitLime just a simple calorie calculator?

No. Calories are only one part of the logic. FitLime also works with goals, daily structure, movement, preferences, and what is sustainable long term.

What does nutrient optimization mean in practice?

It means finding a distribution of energy and macronutrients that fits the goal while still working in real life. Correct numbers alone are not enough if the plan is not livable day to day.

A similar principle in mathematics is known as optimization in a constrained space. It was described as early as the 1940s in the Stigler diet problem, where the goal was to find the cheapest combination of foods that still met nutritional requirements.

FitLime uses the same logic, but instead of minimizing cost, it optimizes a combination of nutrients, daily structure, and practical usability.

Mathematical formula for the curious

The formula looks complex, but in simple terms the system is searching for a solution that maximizes "plan quality" while satisfying all nutritional and practical constraints.

Why is one universal template not enough for everyone?

Because the same recommendation will not work equally well for someone with a sedentary routine, an athlete, or a person with an irregular work schedule. Personalization is what solves that gap.

Why a personalized meal plan works better

Universal nutrition plans often fail because they ignore the variability of human biorhythm and lifestyle. Differences in working hours, training intensity, or sleep quality fundamentally affect how the body processes nutrients and recovers.

A personalized meal plan from FitLime connects these factors into one functional whole. Instead of strict restrictions, it offers a logical structure that adapts to your day. By respecting your habits and physiological needs, the plan reduces the risk of relapse and uncontrolled cravings, which is key to long-term success without the feeling of constant limitation.

Common questions about diet optimization

How do you build a meal plan for weight loss?

An effective weight-loss meal plan has to be built around a controlled calorie deficit, while still being moderate enough to protect metabolic health and lean body mass. FitLime monitors sufficient protein and fiber intake, both of which improve satiety. The system also places carbohydrates strategically around physical activity so the body can use fat stores without sacrificing energy.

How many calories should I eat per day?

Ideal calorie intake is highly individual and depends on age, sex, body composition, and daily activity level (PAL). FitLime uses these variables to calculate a precise energy range in which you can reach your goal most efficiently. We also update that target regularly based on your feedback and progress, which helps prevent stagnation caused by adaptation.

What is the ideal ratio of protein, fats, and carbs?

There is no single fixed macronutrient ratio that works for everyone. An endurance athlete may benefit from a higher share of carbohydrates to restore glycogen, while strength-focused training or a sedentary job may call for a somewhat higher share of fats and protein. FitLime adjusts these ratios dynamically depending on the activity planned for that day so the nutrient profile always matches the body's current demand.

How does a personalized meal plan work?

It works as a bridge between nutrition science and your digital questionnaire. The system goes through thousands of ingredient combinations and selects the ones that best fit your micro- and macronutrient goals. The result is a personalized meal plan that knows exactly how many grams of each food you need to cover vitamins, minerals, and energy, without forcing you to manually count calories in apps.
