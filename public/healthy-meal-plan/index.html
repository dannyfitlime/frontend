Why personalization is more valuable than generic rules

Generic advice often ignores the part that matters most: students, parents, office workers, and active people all live differently. A healthy meal plan only works when it respects what you can realistically follow, what you enjoy eating, and how your day actually looks. That is why FitLime does not rely on universal templates, but on a practical personalized approach.

Useful questions

Is a healthy meal plan only for athletes?

No. It is often even more useful for people who simply want to eat more sensibly and feel better throughout the day.

Can I want a healthy meal plan without wanting weight loss?

Yes. Better energy, routine, easier decisions, or support for an active lifestyle are all valid goals.

What if I have my own food preferences?

That is exactly why the input form exists. A practical plan has to match what you can follow long term.
